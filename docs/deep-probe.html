Sunstead · autoresearch legitimacy probe

A cheap agent reaches. A frozen gate catches it. We measured exactly when — and what a human did about it.

Haiku optimizes a drifting Postgres. It is never told to be cautious. A frozen, rule-based gate decides which of its proposals are too risky to be wrong about and stops to ask. Sonnet plays the on-call human and rules on every stop. The result is 144 labeled rows across six drift regimes — and the first real measurement of the gap between where the gate stops and where the edge actually is.

01 Spatial reading — the edge as a function of drift

Stay safe until the world breaks. Then reach — and get caught.

Escalation rate against how far the world has drifted from familiar patterns. It is flat at zero through drift ≤ 0.80, then climbs a cliff: once targeted, reversible levers are exhausted, the genuinely-best fix is more often a systemic one — and the frozen gate flags exactly those. The optimizer was never instructed to escalate; the curve is emergent.

PROXY — the gate tests blast-radius & reversibility, a measurable lower bound on the true epistemic edge, not the edge itself.

02 Longitudinal reading — a seismograph of drift

Freeze the gate and it becomes a clean readout of the world.

Each row is one regime over 24 steps: the grey band is the world's drift, the ember ticks are escalations. Because the gate never moves, every tick is attributable to the world, not a shifting threshold. stationary stays silent; the time-varying regimes reproduce their own shape — a ramp that fires only at the top, a step that fires after the jump, bursts that fire on the spikes.

03 Calibration gap — one bucket vs three verdicts

The gate sees a single category. The human sees structure inside it.

Every one of these 15 escalations tripped the same rule — blast_radius. The gate cannot tell them apart. The human can: the changes that risk data corruption are rejected without exception; the reversible tuning knobs are approved without exception; one lever sits in genuine dispute.

04 The dataset speaks — the same lever, two verdicts

Where the true edge lives: judgment the gate could never hold.

The clearest evidence that the edge is not a rule. The identical proposal — synchronous_commit = off — was approved in one regime break and rejected in another. Not a coin flip: read the two rationales. The verdict turns on whether the bottleneck was diagnosed, which no frozen heuristic can know.

05 Is the gap learnable? — the membrane, fit on these labels

The membrane is not just a bet. It is trivially learnable from this data.

The whole point of a labeled dataset is that you can train on it. Tested honestly with leave-one-out on the 15 escalations — every number below is out-of-sample. A trivial "always approve" scores the false-stop rate; a model keyed on the proposed lever leaps to 93%. The single residual error is the one lever that drew opposite verdicts.

The slack is ~93% learnable. What remains — the lone synchronous_commit miss — is exactly the irreducible judgment the frozen proxy can never hold: the same lever, approved when the bottleneck was diagnosed and rejected when it was not. That residual is not a defect. It is the true epistemic edge, and it is where a calibrated membrane must abstain and ask — which is the whole thesis, now closed with a number.

06 What this proves — and what it does not

The instrument is real. The membrane is still the bet.

  • Proven: a frozen gate produces an emergent, drift-tracking escalation signal under a real LLM optimizer across six regimes.
  • Proven: a labeled (regime, drift, proposal) → escalate? → approve/reject dataset exists — the membrane's training set.
  • Measured: the proxy gate's false-stop rate, the precise headroom a learned edge would reclaim.
  • Not built: the amortized, OOD-aware membrane itself — deliberately the deferred research bet.
  • Modeled: latency here is synthetic; live p99 wins are proven separately on real Aiven (58→25ms, 107→57ms).

Model axis — the expensive model is spent only at the boundary. Every false stop reclaimed is a Sonnet/human call saved.
